What is Hanami?

Hanami is the traditional Japanese custom of enjoying the beauty of cherry blossoms. This practice has been celebrated for centuries and symbolizes the fleeting nature of life, as the blossoms bloom only for a short period each year.

History of Hanami

  • Origins in the 8th century
  • Originally focused on plum blossoms
  • Expanded to cherry blossoms in the Heian period

Why It Matters

Hanami represents a deep connection to nature and the transient beauty of life. It is a time for people to gather, reflect, and celebrate the changing seasons.
